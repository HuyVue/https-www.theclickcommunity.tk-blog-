Photoshop Friday: adding a warm summer glow

130 Comments / Editing Tutorial / By Leah Cook
Share12
Tweet
Pin7.9K
7.9K Shares

1. I’ve made basic edits to this image in ACR including adjusting white balance, tweak exposure, and recover a bit of the white shirt.  After pulling it into Photoshop, I corrected some yellow-green color casts and that was it!

2. Next I created a Selective Color layer and switched it to the neutrals box.  Here I’ve decreased the magenta to -2 which helps add a little warmth.  I haven’t touched the Cyan value, but that one can also be adjusted.

3. Then I created a Levels layer and found some ‘numbers’ that worked to add the summery glow.  Aside from changing the output level to 40 in RGB, I’ve also used the pull down to select Red and Blue (Green did not change.)

4. I’ve decreased the opacity on the Levels layer to 80%

5. Deciding that the image needed a little pop, I’ve created a Brightness/Contrast layer: Brightness +2 and Contrast +10.

6. I’ve then lightly masked their beautiful faces to pull off some of the “glow” so that they still shine through.

7. Lastly, I create another layer and burn some of the edges to define the image, nothing drastic.
