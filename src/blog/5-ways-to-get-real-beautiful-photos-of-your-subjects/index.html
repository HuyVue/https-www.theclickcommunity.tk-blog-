5 ways to get real, beautiful photos of your subjects

104 Comments / Photography Tutorial / By Michelle Gifford
Share139
Tweet
Pin34
173 Shares

So you’re a photographer, and if you’re like me, one of the things you love about this profession is having the opportunity to capture the real beauty and personality of a person.

Sometimes accomplishing that goal is simple. But honestly, many times achieving real and honest portraits can be a bit of a challenge.

Well, I’ve got some tips to help you capture the true beauty out of every subject.

1. Get to know your subjects.

Before the session, get to know your subject as much as you can. You can do this through an emailed questionnaire, a one-on-one consultation or through a phone conversation. Make sure you are asking good questions that give you insight to what makes this person who they are. When communicating with them, show your personality as much as possible. The more they know you, the more comfortable they will be during their photo shoot.

2. Get in authority mode.

Let’s face it. We don’t photograph many models. These are regular, beautiful people, and they don’t know how to pose in front of a camera. Put that big lens in front of their face, and more than likely, they’re going to feel awkward and uncomfortable. The best way to alleviate this is to make sure your subject knows that you will take care of them. At the beginning of each session, I tell my clients not to worry because I’m a professional and it’s my job to make sure that they look their best. This instantly puts them at ease because it takes the responsibility off of their shoulders.  It lets them know that I’ve got everything under control.

3. Get personal.

Create a session that is all about them. Bring in props or select a location that is consistent with who they are. This familiarity will help them feel relaxed. When your subjects feel this, it will show on their faces and in their subtle mannerisms. You will know what props and location to use because you have gotten to know your them (remember tip #1!).

4. Get the right angles.

You’re the professional, right? That means you need to know your stuff. You need to know the right angles that will be the most flattering for your subject. Study these angles and execute them well.

The angle considerations to always keep in mind are:

  1. Shoot from above if possible, even if just slightly.
  2. Have them put their weight on their back leg and bend their front one.
  3. Turn your subject slightly towards the camera so their shoulders are not facing straight on, but are not completely sideways either.

5. Get real expressions.

There is no simpler way to capture your subjects real beauty than to capture their real expressions. Make jokes that get them to laugh. Give them prompts to get them to have real movement and real expressions. The easiest way to get expressions is to have them interact with others: their friends, children or spouse. Nothing is more beautiful than real, absolute love.

So, there you have it! Five guaranteed ways to get more beautiful pictures from your subjects. And if you really want to make your subjects feel beautiful, join the movement Beauty Revived. Beauty Revived is a site dedicated to tell the stories of real women with real beauty. Photographers donate a session to women who show attributes of real beauty.
