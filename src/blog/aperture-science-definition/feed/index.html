It’s a bit tricky – technically the focal length isn’t a consideration in the exposure triangle, hence why it’s written as f/2.8 instead of 50/2.8 or 85/2.8. Say I’m shooting with my 70-200mm lens. I take a photo at 70mm f/2.8. Then I take another image at 200mm f/2.8. The exposure of the image will remain the same without adjusting any of the settings because the camera has made the aperture opening larger as I zoomed in. Even though the opening diameter will be larger when it’s shot at 200mm, in the end the exposure is the same as the image shot at 70mm. Hopefully this makes sense – give it a try on your camera and see it happen!
